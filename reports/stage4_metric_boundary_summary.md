# Stage 4 Summary: Metric Boundary and Behavioral KL

Status: **complete 2026-07-04**. Details are in
[`docs/experiment_log.md`](../docs/experiment_log.md), from "Stage 4A
pre-registration" through the Stage 4C results.

## Question

Stages 2/3 established that rel-hat is a strong local sparse-attention scorer.
Stage 4 asks:

```text
Does local attention-output error also serve as a model-behavior metric?
```

Answer: **only up to W_O projection. Next-token KL breaks the local-oracle
reference.**

## Stage 4A: W_O-Projected Error

Stage 4A evaluates errors after each head contribution is projected through
the model's attention output projection.

Result:

```text
BERT rel-hat mean gap: 0.790 -> 0.755
GPT-2 rel-hat mean gap: 0.828 -> 0.702
```

The scorer ladder survives, but attenuates. This rules out the strongest
negative story ("we optimized a completely wrong local direction") while
warning that downstream metrics may further compress the advantage.

## Stage 4B: GPT-2 Single-Head Next-Token KL

Protocol:

```text
For each doc/layer/head/budget:
  replace one head's attention context with sparse context
  continue the real GPT-2 forward pass
  compare sparse vs dense next-token distributions
```

Implementation guard:

```text
dense manual reconstruction:
layer 0  max logit diff 0.00387, mean 8.7e-05
layer 5  max logit diff 0.00066, mean 2.3e-05
layer 11 max logit diff 0.00012, mean 7.2e-06
```

This check caught an early raw-V patching bug before the full run.

Per-configuration mean-KL improvement:

```text
projected_oracle  -0.407
mass              -0.083
UTC-abs           -0.042
UTC-rel           -0.205
UTC-rel-hat       -0.091
```

Aggregate mean-KL reduction:

```text
projected_oracle  -0.192
mass              -0.229
UTC-abs            0.146
UTC-rel            0.057
UTC-rel-hat        0.183
```

Key observation: the W_O-projected local oracle is worse than fixed under KL.
This means the local gap-closed axis is not a behavioral oracle axis.

## Stage 4C: GPT-2 Whole-Layer KL

Single-head KL could be too weak and redundancy-dominated, so Stage 4C patches
all heads in one layer simultaneously.

Signal size improves:

```text
Stage 4B single-head fixed mean KL: 0.00303
Stage 4C whole-layer fixed mean KL: 0.02578
```

But the local scorer ladder does not reappear.

Per-configuration mean-KL improvement:

```text
projected_oracle  -1.554
mass              -0.146
UTC-abs            0.096
UTC-rel           -0.262
UTC-rel-hat       -0.026
```

Aggregate mean-KL reduction:

```text
projected_oracle  -0.527
mass              -0.193
UTC-abs            0.069
UTC-rel           -0.049
UTC-rel-hat        0.074
```

The single-head concern was real, but insufficient: even under deployment-shaped
whole-layer intervention, local W_O/head-space oracles remain poor behavioral
oracles.

## Depth Gradient

Whole-layer KL exposes a structured boundary:

```text
aggregate mean-KL reduction by layer:

layer 0:
  UTC-rel-hat       +0.114
  projected_oracle  -0.194

layer 5:
  UTC-rel-hat       -0.051
  projected_oracle  -0.032

layer 11:
  UTC-rel-hat       -0.205
  projected_oracle  -3.194
```

The boundary is not random. Local fidelity remains more useful early, then
becomes increasingly misaligned near the readout structure.

## Final Boundary Claim

The project should not claim end-to-end sparse-attention success. The correct
claim is narrower and stronger:

```text
Value-aware local sparse-attention error can be controlled cheaply and
transfers through W_O with attenuation, but local restricted oracles are not
behavioral oracles under next-token KL.
```

## Caveats and Non-Claims

- No KL-aware oracle is built in this stage.
- No perplexity or task metric improvement is claimed.
- No sparse kernel or wall-clock speedup is claimed.
- The KL experiments are still small-scale: GPT-2 small, 3 documents,
  layers 0/5/11, budgets 8/16/32.

## What Would Be Next

Further work should be treated as a new project:

- construct a behavioral / KL oracle;
- add position, depth, direction, and readout-aware importance weighting;
- broaden model/data coverage;
- move from top-k-by-probability to subset or block selection;
- implement sparse kernels and measure wall-clock effects.

Those are deliberately outside this artifact's scope.
